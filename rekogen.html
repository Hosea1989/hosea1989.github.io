The Problem

As an anime fan, I constantly struggled with the overwhelming question: "What should I watch next?" With thousands of anime series available across multiple platforms, finding something that matches your specific taste in genres, pacing, and mood can be incredibly frustrating.

Existing recommendation systems are often too generic or don't account for the nuanced preferences that make anime enjoyment so personal.

The Solution

RekoGen solves this through a comprehensive onboarding quiz that analyzes your preferences across multiple dimensions - not just genres, but also pacing, themes, and viewing context.

By combining data from three major anime APIs (Jikan, Kitsu, and AniList), RekoGen provides highly personalized recommendations while also connecting you with a community of like-minded fans.

Why I Built RekoGen

As a lifelong anime fan, I've experienced the frustration of finishing an amazing series and spending hours trying to find something equally captivating. Existing platforms either had limited recommendations or didn't understand the nuanced preferences that make anime so personal.

The Technical Challenge: Building RekoGen required integrating three different anime APIs (Jikan, Kitsu, and AniList), each with unique data structures and rate limits. The real challenge was creating a unified experience that leverages the strengths of each platform while maintaining fast performance.

The Community Aspect: What started as a personal project evolved into something bigger when I realized other fans shared the same struggles. The community realms feature came from beta tester feedback - they wanted to connect with people who shared their specific anime interests.

RekoGen combines data from three major sources: Jikan (MyAnimeList), Kitsu, and AniList. This gives us access to over 50,000 anime titles with comprehensive information including ratings, genres, episode counts, and community discussions.

Our recommendation engine analyzes your viewing history, ratings, and preferences across multiple dimensions including genres, themes, pacing, and mood. We also factor in community trends and similar user preferences to suggest anime you're likely to enjoy.

Community Realms are themed discussion spaces where fans can connect around specific anime, genres, or topics. Each realm has its own feed, recommendations, and community-driven content. It's like having specialized forums for every anime interest!
